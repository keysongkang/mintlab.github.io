Research at MINT Lab

From microscopic interactions to AI-driven materials discovery.

We combine first-principles theory, atomistic simulation, transport modeling, and materials AI to understand emergent properties and discover next-generation functional materials.

Core Concept

Electron · Lattice · Spin · Ion

Many advanced materials are governed by coupled microscopic degrees of freedom. MINT Lab investigates how these interactions create macroscopic functionality such as memory, energy conversion, transport, and switching.

Research Areas

Three connected research directions.

Our projects connect fundamental materials physics with computational design and AI-accelerated workflows.

01

Magnetic Memory & Spintronics

We study metallic antiferromagnets, spin-related transport, and microscopic mechanisms for next-generation magnetic memory and low-power devices.

02

Thermoelectric Materials

We predict thermal and electronic transport properties to identify high-performance thermoelectric and doped semiconductor materials.

03

AI-Accelerated Simulation

We develop machine-learning potentials, deep Hamiltonian models, and active-learning workflows to accelerate expensive materials simulations.

Workflow

Discovery loop.

We use simulation and AI not as separate tools, but as a closed-loop framework for generating, testing, and refining materials hypotheses.

1

Theory

Identify mechanisms, descriptors, and design principles from microscopic physics.

2

Simulation

Predict structure, stability, transport, phonon, magnon, and electronic properties.

3

AI Models

Train machine-learning models to accelerate costly calculations and explore larger materials spaces.

4

Collaboration

Work closely with experimental groups to validate predictions and refine design strategies.

Current Focus

Materials problems we care about.

Magnetic materials

Metallic antiferromagnets for next-generation memory

We investigate fundamental magnetic, thermal, and electronic properties of metallic antiferromagnets and related spintronic materials.

Energy materials

High-performance thermoelectric materials

We model electronic and phonon transport to discover materials for waste-heat recovery and energy-efficient technologies.

Methods

Reliable AI workflows for materials simulation

We build data-efficient, physically grounded AI workflows that speed up molecular dynamics, transport calculations, and materials screening.
